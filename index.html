Stemness Regenerative Project

Ahmed A Abdi

Stem cell biology represents a fundamental area of developmental biology with significant implications for regenerative medicine and evolutionary studies. While mammalian stem cell systems have been extensively characterized through the identification of core transcription factors (Oct3/4, Sox2, Klf4, c-Myc) that induce pluripotency (Takahashi & Yamanaka, 2006), the evolutionary conservation of these mechanisms across metazoans remains poorly understood.

Recent studies in non-model organisms have revealed surprising divergence in stem cell regulatory networks. Research in the cnidarian Hydra has identified distinct genetic modules maintaining stemness (Hemmrich et al., 2012), while investigations in glossiphoniid leeches (Helobdella austinensis) have revealed unique sets of nucleic acid binding proteins associated with teloblast formation that show minimal similarity to known mammalian or cnidarian stemness factors (Hsiao et al., 2024).
